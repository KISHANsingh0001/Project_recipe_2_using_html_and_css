Gulab Jamun

Ingredients For Gulab jamun balls:

  • 1 cup full-fat milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • A pinch of baking soda
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/3 cup milk (approximately)
  • A few saffron strands (optional)
  • Chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios) for garnishing (optional)

For the Sugar Syrup(Gulab)

  • 1.5 cups granulated sugar
  • 1.5 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1 tablespoon rose water

Recipe

Prepare the Sugar Syrup (Gulab):

  1. In a saucepan, combine sugar and water.
  2. Bring it to a boil over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Add cardamom powder and rose water to the syrup and let it simmer for 5-6 minutes until it slightly thickens.
    Then, turn off the heat. Keep the syrup warm while you make the Gulab Jamun balls.

Prepare the Gulab Jamun balls:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, baking powder, and baking soda.
  2. If using saffron strands, crush them and add to the mixture.
  3. Add ghee and mix well. The mixture should resemble bread crumbs.
  4. Slowly add milk little by little, and knead the mixture into a soft, smooth, and slightly sticky dough.
    Add milk cautiously, as you might not need the entire amount mentioned in the ingredients list.
  5. Cover the dough and let it rest for 5-10 minutes.

Shape the Gulab Jamun balls:

  1. Divide the dough into small, smooth balls without any cracks. Make sure they are compact and smooth. If the dough feels too sticky, grease your palms with ghee while shaping the balls.

Fry the Gulab Jamun balls:

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a deep pan or skillet over low-medium heat. The oil/ghee should be moderately hot, not too hot, or too cool.
  2. Fry the Gulab Jamun balls in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan. Fry until they turn golden brown, stirring gently for even cooking. This may take around 5-7 minutes per batch.
  3. Remove the fried Gulab Jamun balls using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil by placing them on a paper towel.

Soak the Gulab Jamun:

  1. While the Gulab Jamun balls are still warm, gently drop them into the warm sugar syrup (Gulab). Ensure the syrup covers the Gulab Jamun entirely.
  2. Let the Gulab Jamun soak in the syrup for at least 1 to 2 hours before serving. During this time, the Gulab Jamun will absorb the syrup and become soft, juicy, and flavorful.

Caprese Salad

Ingredients

  • Fresh tomatoes (sliced)
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese (sliced)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Balsamic glaze or balsamic vinegar
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe

  1. Arrange the tomato and mozzarella slices on a serving plate, alternating between them.
  2. Tuck fresh basil leaves between the tomato and mozzarella slices.
  3. Drizzle balsamic glaze or balsamic vinegar and extra virgin olive oil over the salad.
  4. Sprinkle salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve immediately as a light and refreshing appetizer or side dish.

Rasmalai

Rasmalai is a popular Indian dessert made from spongy cottage cheese dumplings soaked in sweet, flavored milk. It's a delightful treat that is perfect for special occasions or whenever you have a craving for something sweet. Here's a simple recipe to make Rasmalai at home:

Ingredients

    For the Rasgulla (cottage cheese dumplings)

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice or white vinegar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water

    • For the Flavored Milk:

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A few strands of saffron
  • 1 tablespoon chopped pistachios or almonds for garnish

Instructions

  1. Making Rasgulla (Cottage Cheese Dumplings):

    • In a large pot, bring 1 liter of milk to a boil. Once it starts boiling, add the lemon juice or
      white vinegar gradually while stirring gently.
    • The milk will curdle, and the whey will separate from the solids (curdled milk). Turn off the heat.
    • Line a strainer with a cheesecloth or muslin cloth and pour the curdled milk through it to separate the
      whey from the cottage cheese. Rinse the cottage cheese
      under cold water to remove any lemony flavor.
    • Squeeze out the excess water from the cottage cheese by gathering the cloth and pressing gently. Hang the cloth for
      about 30 minutes to drain the remaining water.
    • After 30 minutes, remove the cottage cheese from the cloth and knead
      it well until it becomes smooth and forms a dough-like consistency.
    • Divide the cottage cheese into small equal-sized balls and press them
      gently to flatten slightly.

  2. Cooking Rasgulla:

    • In a wide pan, combine 4 cups of water and 1 cup of sugar. Bring it to a boil, ensuring the sugar dissolves completely.
    • Carefully drop the cottage cheese balls into the boiling sugar syrup.
    • Cover the pan with a lid and let the Rasgullas cook on medium heat for about 15-20 minutes. They will puff up during the cooking process.
    • Once the Rasgullas are cooked, they will float to the surface. Turn off the heat and let them cool for a few minutes.

  3. Preparing Flavored Milk (Ras):

    • In another saucepan, heat 1 liter of milk. Stir it occasionally to avoid burning or sticking to the bottom.
    • Add sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron strands to the milk. Mix well and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes until it reduces slightly and thickens.

  4. Soaking the Rasgullas:

    • Gently squeeze the syrup from the cooked Rasgullas.
    • Transfer the Rasgullas to the prepared flavored milk (Ras). Allow them to soak for at least 2 hours in the milk to absorb the flavors.

  5. Serving Rasmalai:

    • Garnish the Rasmalai with chopped pistachios or almonds.
    • Serve chilled for the best taste and experience.
